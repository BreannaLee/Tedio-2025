How to do it

  1. Read the title aloud (with parents’ help depending on the age)
  2. What is this video probably about?
  3. Why do I want to click on this video?
  4. Do I see any sign of untrustworthiness or danger from the Thumbnail or the creator?

Tips

Frame this exercise as a cool brain challenge.

Teach children about the value of intentionality. If kids learn and train practicing intention from a young age, this could significantly impact their social media viewing habits (what am I viewing and why do I want to view this?) and could help build life-long muscle to think intentionally.

Quick Exercise

After seeing the above thumbnail for 10s, discuss:

  1. Title Time: 🗣️ “Let’s read the title together. What words jump out?” (Parent reads if needed.)
  2. Guess the Story: 🧠 “Take one guess: ‘I think this video will show…’”
  3. Check Your Why: ✅ “Why do you want to watch it? Curious? Bored? Excited?”
  4. Safety Scan: ⛑️ “Look at the picture. Any uh-oh signs (ex. scary faces, ALL-CAPS, ‘Prank gone wrong’)? Thumbs-up if safe, thumbs-down if not sure.”
  5. Decide Together: ‍⚖️ “Watch now, watch with a parent, or swipe away?”
